The App

Everything a beekeeper needs

From first-time hobbyist to commercial operation, ApiScan grows with you — and your data grows with the planet.

📱

QR Scan & Go

Stick a QR code on each hive. Scan it on arrival and jump straight into your inspection — no searching, no typos, no forgotten hive names.

📊

Smart Tracking

Track brood frames, varroa counts, queen status, honey stores, and hive mood over time. Spot trends before they become problems.

🌍

Global Data Pool

Your anonymized data contributes to a global picture of hive health — shared with researchers studying colony collapse and disease spread.

📈

Trend Analysis

Visualize varroa counts across seasons, compare hive health across your apiary, and follow your colony's development in beautiful charts.

🖨️

QR Batch Generation

Generate batches of up to 50 QR codes and download a print-ready PDF — perfect for labelling a new apiary or replacing lost tags.

🔒

Your Data, Private

Precise locations and personal notes stay private. Only anonymized aggregates are shared — you control what's visible on the public map.

Why It Matters

One third of everything you eat depends on bees

Honey bees and wild pollinators fertilize roughly 35 % of the world's food supply — fruits, vegetables, nuts, and oils. But populations have been declining for decades, and science is still scrambling to understand exactly why.

  • Colony Collapse Disorder affects millions of hives every year
  • Varroa mite is the #1 threat to managed bee colonies globally
  • Pesticides, habitat loss, and climate change compound the pressure
  • Real-time beekeeper data is essential for early-warning systems

When you log an inspection in ApiScan, you're not just managing your hive — you're adding a data point to a global early-warning network for bee health.

Collective Intelligence

Why community data changes everything

A single beekeeper sees one apiary. Together, we see the world — and we can do something about it.

Disease Mapping

Identify geographic clusters of varroa infestation or disease outbreaks before they spread, enabling targeted beekeeper intervention across regions.

Seasonal Trends

Cross-reference hive health with weather patterns, bloom calendars, and local land-use to pinpoint what's driving colony losses in each area.

Research Partnership

Our anonymized dataset is shared with university researchers and conservation NGOs, contributing to peer-reviewed science on pollinator health.

Policy Change

Hard data from thousands of apiaries gives beekeepers a powerful evidence-based voice in conversations about pesticide regulation and habitat protection.
